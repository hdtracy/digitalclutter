Selective exposure has also been known and defined as "congeniality bias" or [[confirmation bias|Confirmation Bias]] in various texts throughout the years.

Selective exposure relies on the assumption that one will continue to seek out information on an issue even after an individual has taken a stance on it. The position that a person has taken will be colored by various factors of that issue that are reinforced during the  [decision-making process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision-making_process "Decision-making process"). According to Stroud (2008), theoretically, selective exposure occurs when people's beliefs guide their media selections.
