 In the book [[No Nonsense Spirituality by Brittney Hartley]], the author describes four existential fears that all humans carry.
## Fear of Death
>"Religion may have been created when we first became smart enough that we knew we were going to die." <cite>Brittney Hartley, No Nonsense Spirituality</cite>

## Fear of Freedom
>"If all cult leaders died today, we would recreate them tomorrow because it's so much easier to give your moral compass and life to someone else who seems trustworthy than to accept that you are ultimately responsible for your own choices." <cite>Brittney Hartley, No Nonsense Spirituality</cite>

Is this why we "resurrected" Jesus? Did our leader die and there was a need to recreate him or rewrite that story so that we could still grant him our freedom?

>"How dismal it is to see present-day Americans yearning for the very orthodoxy that their country was founded to escape." <cite>Christopher Hitchens</cite>

## Fear of Isolation
>"Loneliness does not come from having no people about one, but from being unable to communicate the things that seem important to oneself, or from holding certain views which others find inadmissible." <cite>Carl Jung</cite> 

## Fear of Meaninglessness
>"There's a part of heaven on earth that only I can build, in my community and family. If I remove myself from this web of life to which I am connected, I become the very monster I was trying to run away from because I become the suffering I was trying to escape. When we lose someone, we really do lose something that matters to the world. There are values, gifts, and a voice in the world that only they have." <cite>Brittney Hartley, No Nonsense Spirituality</cite>

>"Secular spirituality does best when it balances order and chaos because it has tools for both building and tearing down. Organized religion only has tools for structure, and skepticism only has tools for tearing down. But the reality is that we need both." <cite>Brittney Hartley, No Nonsense Spirituality</cite>

>"The paradox I've found is that the most spiritual atheist and the most deconstructed religious believer find themselves in a similar place." <cite>Brittney Hartley, No Nonsense Spirituality</cite>
