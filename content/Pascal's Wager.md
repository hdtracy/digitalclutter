# Pascal’s Wager
[[Pascal’s Wager]] argues that a rational person should live as though God exists and seek to believe in God. 

If God does not exist, such a person will have only a finite loss (some pleasures, luxury, etc.), whereas if God does exist, he stands to receive infinite gains (as represented by eternity in Heaven) and avoid infinite losses (an eternity in Hell).

The original wager was set out in Pascal's posthumously published Pensées ("Thoughts"), an assembly of previously unpublished notes. Pascal's wager charted new territory in probability theory, marked the first formal use of decision theory, existentialism, pragmatism, and voluntarism.

The wager is commonly criticized with counterarguments such as the failure to prove the existence of God, the argument from inconsistent revelations, and the argument from inauthentic belief.

Pascal’s Wager does not argue for the existence of God, instead it argues only for the belief in God. 


### The Wager
The wager uses the following logic (excerpts from Pensées, part III, §233):

>[!quote]- The Wager’s Statements
>- God is, or God is not. Reason cannot decide between the two alternatives
>- A Game is being played... where heads or tails will turn up
>- You must wager (it is not optional)
>- Let us weigh the gain and the loss in wagering that God is. Let us estimate these two chances. If you gain, you gain all; if you lose, you lose nothing
>- Wager, then, without hesitation that He is. (...) There is here an infinity of an infinitely happy life to gain, a chance of gain against a finite number of chances of loss, and what you stake is finite. And so our proposition is of infinite force when there is the finite to stake in a game where there are equal risks of gain and of loss, and the infinite to gain.
>- But some cannot believe. They should then 'at least learn your inability to believe...' and 'Endeavour then to convince' themselves.


### Is there nothing to lose if you wager wrong? 
- A life lived without guilt for thought crime
- Rules around intimacy and who you are able to associate with closely
- People shunned, discriminated against based on beliefs 


### Challenges
- Pascal’s wager doesn’t specify a particular God. The same wager could be used between the Christian faith and the Muslim faith.
- You can’t choose what to believe in, you can’t change reality. You must be convinced something is actually true to believe it.
