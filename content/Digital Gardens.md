**Digital Gardens** are a place to sort, process, tend, and grow your knowledge on a variety of topics. They can be weeded, pruned, watered, and cared for similar to an outdoor garden.

Digital gardens attempt to counteract the temporary and inherently fleeting flow of information we encounter in daily life. [[Maggie Appleton]] refers back to [[Caufield]] who calls these **streams**. The idea of streams describe the "collapse of information into single track timelines of events." Appleton writes, "*streams only surface the Zeitgeisty ideas of the last 24 hours. They are not designed to accumulate knowledge, connect disparate information, or mature over time.*" 

Digital Gardens are also associated with movements such as [[Learning in Public]].
