First heard about from [[content/People/Nicole van der Hoeven\|Nicole van der Hoeven]] in her video about [[Learning New Things in Obsidian (2024) In Defense of Remembering\|Learning New Things in Obsidian (2024) In Defense of Remembering]]. She describes it as a method she uses when processing her [[literature notes\|literature notes]]:

>"So it consists of three things: **thesis**, **antithesis**, and **synthesis**. So the idea is that in **thesis**, you talk about the things that the author was trying to say, their main points, and then in **antithesis**, you talk about the things that maybe they get wrong, or other author's points that counter these ones, and then in **synthesis**, you try to think about how they can come together."

According to dictionary.com, it defines the Hegelian Dialectic as "*an interpretive method in which the contradiction between a proposition (thesis) and its antithesis is resolved at a higher level of truth (synthesis).*"
