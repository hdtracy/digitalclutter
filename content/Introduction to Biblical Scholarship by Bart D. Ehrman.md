[[Martin Luther]] (1483-1546 CE) insisted on a literal interpretation of scripture, a concept known as "[[Sola Scriptura]]." 

After printing was invented in the 15th century, the question arose, which version of all these manuscripts do we print? The first person to print a new testament for use was [[Erasmus]] in 1516, who was a Greek scholar.
	He used two primary manuscripts and printed them directly. The manuscript of Revelation was missing the last page. 
This version became the groundwork for modern versions such as the King James Version. 

[[Textual Criticism]] - determining the original words of a text
	Just knowing the original words of a text doesn't necessarily make them true or accurate.

### What are our oldest sources?
- John and the Synoptic gospels
	- John's gospel differs greatly from the synoptics. Scholars begin to believe John didn't use Matthew, Mark, and Luke as sources and was in fact the last/latest gospel. 
	- Matthew and Luke appear to have used Mark as a source. 
		- Mark has a concept known as the [[Messianic Secret]], an idea that Jesus was trying to keep his savior status a secret
- Most conservative estimate gives a gap of at least 35-40 years between Jesus's death and the appearance of our first source
