## About

A list of helpful questions for Street Epistemology found posted by Redditor, they remark it is primarily inspired by Anthony Magnabosco's tweets. 
https://github.com/lumpiluk/sequestions?tab=readme-ov-file

## The Questions
- If you discovered that your belief was causing harm, would you be more open to re-examining it for accuracy?
- If this wasn't true, would you want to know that?
- Can people be mistaken about their memories/experiences?
- Is everything you've been taught true? (Has everything you've been taught been true?)
- Do beliefs that give comfort make them true?
- Are feelings the best way to know if something is true?
- How could a person test that prayer actually works?
- When did you last honestly question your God belief?
- What if you were taught something that wasn't true?
- Can doubt ever be beneficial?
- Does one need a God belief in order to be good?
- If your God belief was mistaken, how would you discover that?
- Would you rather have evidence or faith for your God belief?
- How does it feel to discover you were mistaken about something?
- If you were raised Christian/Muslim/Hindu/Pagan/atheist/…, would you believe it today?
- Is a belief true simply because it is old?
- Is a belief true because lots of other people hold it?
- Does prayer always work?
- What does a failed prayer look like?
- How could a person build a test for karma/miracles/prayer/a God/…?
- What would it take to discard your belief?
- Is holding this belief more important to you than the belief being true?
- Would your belief change if or when we discovered how to extend our lives almost indefinitely?
- If you were raised to not believe that there was a god and you had these experiences, for such a person, what do you think the takeaway would have been in that situation?
- How do you differentiate between your own independent thoughts and your belief that a God is speaking to you?
- Could we be mistaken today like ancient Romans or Greeks were mistaken about their gods? Is there a way to minimize the risk?
- What does a world where X isn't the case look like?
- If ten minutes from now X stopped being the case, what would change?
- If you were living in a world where X wasn't the case, what would you be looking for to discover that?
